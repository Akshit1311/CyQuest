Rules And Regulations


Each team will receive a three-part problem statement.

The equipment allowed includes only laptops and basic support equipment (mice or keyboards). Participants are to rely only on their laptops to complete the tests and external graphics cards are not allowed.

Participants have to create up to half of their implementation before the competition.

Mentors from GDG and FDC will grade the participants based on their progress.

A pop-up quiz via google forms will be held during the hackathon and the winning teams will be provided with goodies.

Every team will receive a Participation Certificate.

Any team found involved in activities such as attacking internal servers, WiFi setup or any such services shall be at once disqualified.

Communication with other teams shall be considered malpractice and both teams shall be disqualified at once.

Interrupting other teams by attacking their progress shall be considered as malpractice and the team shall be disqualified at once.

The evaluation will be carried out as follows:
a. The base score will be counted based on the test cases presented by the team.
b. Documentation and presentation have extra points counted towards the base score.
c. In case of a tie, the decision of the judges will be final.
